Nigerian Jollof Rice

A rich, smoky, and deeply flavoured one-pot rice dish that is the heart of West African cooking. Perfect for parties, family dinners, or any time you want something truly special.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 50 minutes
  • Servings: 6
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

A Look at the Dish

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of rice
  • 4 large tomatoes, blended
  • 3 tatashe, blended
  • 2 ata rodo, blended
  • 1 large onions, chopped
  • Visually meaasured vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken and vegetable stock
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and seasoning cubes to taste

Instructions

  1. Make the tomato base: Blend the tomatoes, tatashe, ata rodo, and half the onion until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Fry the base: Heat oil in a wide, heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add the remaining chopped onion and fry until golden. Pour in the blended tomato mixture and cook, stirring frequently, for 25–30 minutes until the raw smell is gone and the paste has darkened and thickened.
  3. Season: Add thyme, bay leaves, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir well and cook for another few minutes.
  4. Add rice and stock: Wash the rice until the water runs clear, then stir it into the pot. Pour in the stock and bring to a boil.
  5. Steam to finish: Reduce heat to the lowest setting, cover the pot tightly with foil then the cover, and cook for 25–30 minutes without lifting the cover. The steam is what cooks the rice through and gives it that signature smoky bottom.
  6. Check and serve: Remove the cover, stir gently from the bottom, and taste for seasoning. Serve hot with fried plantain, coleslaw, or grilled chicken.

Tips & Notes

  • The "party" smoky flavour — called Party Jollof — comes from letting the bottom of the rice catch slightly at the very end. Don't be afraid of it.
  • For a richer colour, add tomato paste when frying the base.
  • Do not open the pot while the rice is steaming — you will lose the steam and the rice will turn out dry.
