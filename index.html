West African Classic

Nigerian
Jollof Rice

A rich, smoky, and deeply flavoured dish that defines West African cuisine. Perfect for parties, family dinners, and special occasions.

Recipe Details
Prep 20 mins
Cook 50 mins
Servings 6
Difficulty Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes, blended
  • 3 red bell peppers (tatashe), blended
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 tsp thyme & 2 bay leaves
  • Salt & seasoning cubes to taste

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, peppers, and onion until smooth.
  2. Fry blended base in hot oil for 25–30 mins until darkened.
  3. Add thyme, bay leaves, seasoning and salt.
  4. Stir in washed rice and pour in stock to just cover.
  5. Cover tightly with foil and lid. Steam on low for 25–30 mins.
  6. Fluff, taste, and serve hot.

Chef's Tips

  • Let the bottom catch slightly — that smoky crust is Party Jollof.
  • Add 1 tbsp tomato paste for a deeper, richer colour.
  • Never lift the lid while steaming — you'll lose the steam.
